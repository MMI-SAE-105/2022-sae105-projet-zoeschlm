TRAVEL THOUGH ROOMS OF THE ORANGERIE AT PARIS

Who has never dreamt of traveling in imaginary ? The Orange Grove national museum of Tuileries in Paris proposes to mix architecture and art in a more restricted setting than the Louvre.

CLAUDE MONET, THE MUSEUM’S MUSE

More known for its huge Monet’s exposition in this specific circular room, the Orange Grove museum brings more and more visitors; Impressionism, post impressionism, carving, shows the versatility of the museum continues to attract curious souls. Emblematic of the building, the oval room is dedicated to Claude Monet’s art pieces. A few days after his death, he gave to the new museum 8 impressionists panels, each placed on walls. This exhibition is the only one that is not temporary and is arranged in this impressive room according to the rays of the Paris sun. Moreover, in its other name, the ”Claude Monet’s museum” was inaugurated in 1927 and makes public access.

CULTURE PLACE AND HISTORY

.The architecture of the museum gives an impression of being on an adventure. With closed corridors leading from room to room. Previously an Orange grove in 1852, the building gives other services for the next generations. In fact, from a simple conservation place for orange trees ordered by Napoleon in the 19 century to an exhibition place for artists, there are a lot of modifications. If current exhibitions are about 30’s century artists, it’s rather impressionistic. But we can also find event performances like dancing shows. Despite its small capacity to hold works and exhibitions, artists like to represent themselves there.

Orangerie museum and other places

1782 The Louvre

Of course, our famous Louvre Museum is not that old. In 1792, when it was founded, it already became the most important building in Paris. Many tourists from all over the world want to see the famous Mona Lisa, by DaVinci

1840 Goya Museum

In 1840 the Goya Museum in Castres opened its doors. Inside the museum, Spanish paintings and works of art blend with the French architecture of the Occitanie region.

1892 MBeaux Arts Museum

1892, in the north of France, Lille opens the doors of the Palais des Beaux Arts. Eugène Delacroix, Théodore Géricault and many others lent their paintings to the 12,000 m2 exhibition space.

2001 Piscine Museum

The atypical shape of the museum over the water is part of the cultural heritage of Roubaix. It presents a majority of sculptures but also decorative creations. Formerly the city's municipal swimming pool, it was transformed into a museum in 2001.

2006 Pompidou Center

The Centre Pompidou, the national art museum in Paris, presents more contemporary works of art than the Louvre.

ART CULTURE AND EXHIBITIONS

Claude Monet is lending his Nympheas to the Musée de l'Orangerie and from 5 October 2022 to 23 February 2023 it is also possible to see other works. The Louis Vuitton’s foundation in Paris offers an exhibition of paintings by Joan Mitchell, an expressionist who studied the works of Monet, her mentor. Many exhibitions are being held for 2023.
