La Jeune Femme et la Mer, or the inspiration source

How about a breath of fresh air to reconnect with your imagination and its benefits? Catherine Meurisse takes us on a journey in search of inspiration.

For artists, “art block” phenomenon isn’t a subject to be taken lightly. By making it the first topic of her second graphic novel La Jeune Femme et la Mer, Catherine Meurisse try to make artists feel less guilty about this fuzzy period.

EVERYTHING FOR THE PLEASURE OF EYES

42 years old author, Catherine Meurisse publish La Jeune femme et la Mer on octobrer 29th, 2021. In this one, she tells us about her journey in search for inspiration in a Kyoto residence, at Japan. Through her illustrations she put herself in scene along with pretty words and metaphors. She explains to us a problem that affects many artists : missing inspiration or “art block”. A comic book as attractive in its story as in its drawings. We notices the combination of watercolors and colored pencils. Illustrations of insane Japanese landscapes as well as flowers, sea, the fields and plains that stand out on every double page. The illustrator reaches a perfect artistic combinaison that makes you want to get into the book. Full colored pages but always with pastel and neutral colors.

“ "It's funny, this chance meeting between two painters at the other end of the world! - At the end of the world, speak for yourself. - Can I see your notebook? - It is empty. Well, it doesn't contain any drawings, only poems of my own composition. I try to paint a woman. The expression of a woman. I admit that, for the moment, I'm out of gas. Painting fails. Only haikus work. In seventeen syllables I can better translate the evanescence of things (...) - What is the evanescence of things? - It is that which is outside the world. The poetry to which I aspire is not that which exhorts earthly passions. Rather, it is the poetry that frees me from trivial concerns and gives me the illusion of leaving this dusty world, if only for a moment. Do you like poetry? - Yes, but I don't read much of it - Often the characteristic of poetry is that it can never leave the world. Especially in the poetry of your home country, the West. Your poetry is based on the human and rarely goes beyond that. - What do you mean "never leave the world"? What about du Bellay? Happy who, like Ulysses, has made a beautiful journey..." - Haha! I'm not talking about that world. The Western poet is too down to earth "I pick chrysanthemums at the foot of the eastern hedge and look serenely at the southern mountains. This landscape is enough to erase the world. There are no neighbors behind the hedge and no friends collecting in the mountains, you know? So don't you feel that you are rising above the fray, that you are cleansing yourself of all material desires? - Are you a painter or a poet? - With my paint box, I walk slowly along the mountain path. Breathing in nature the world of the poets, I walk, if only for a moment, in the impassive universe. This is intoxication. "
Extract of the graphic novel La Jeune Femme et la Mer - Catherine Meurisse, 2021

PRETTY CURE TO FIND OURSELVES THROUGH PAGES

The subject she is proposing is far away from light, when we are artists. If from the outside it looks like a simple travel story, it does the heart good of any content creator. Through the meeting of an old man and a raccoon, she reconstructs the path of an artistic reflection. The message she wants to pass : There is no big deal! Sometimes, to create, we need to be alone and absorb what you need to get inspired again. Perhaps this book has finally given some people a break?

TRAVELING BY STAYING IN OUR CHAIR

The whole book based on the legend of the young woman on the bank makes us discover at the same time the countryside of Kyoto and its monuments. On a more instructive note, she evokes the difference of cultures between Europe and Asia. Especially in literature, paintings, legends and ways of living together
